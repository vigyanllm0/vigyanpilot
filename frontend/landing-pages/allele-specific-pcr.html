Frequently Asked Questions: allele-specific PCR

How does allele-specific PCR work?

Allele-specific PCR (also called ARMS-PCR or AS-PCR) discriminates between alleles by placing the discriminatory nucleotide at the 3' end of the primer. DNA polymerase extends efficiently only when there is perfect complementarity at the 3' end, so the primer amplifies one allele but not the other. VigyanLLM designs allele-specific primers with optimized 3' positioning, introduces intentional mismatches near the 3' end to enhance specificity, and validates the assay design against reference genome databases.
