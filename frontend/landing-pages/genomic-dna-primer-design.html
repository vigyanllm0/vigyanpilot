Intron-Aware Design for Genomic Templates

Designing primers for genomic DNA requires understanding the complete gene structure. VigyanLLM maps exon-intron boundaries from reference databases and designs primers that either target single exons (for short amplicons) or span introns (for genomic DNA-specific detection). This intron-aware approach ensures your primers work correctly on genomic DNA templates without being confused by processed pseudogenes or cDNA contamination.

Frequently Asked Questions: genomic DNA primer design

How are genomic DNA primers different from cDNA primers?

Genomic DNA primers must account for introns — long non-coding sequences within genes that are absent from processed mRNA. When designing gDNA primers, VigyanLLM considers the full gene structure including introns, ensures primers don't span splice junctions (which would fail on genomic DNA), and designs intron-spanning amplicons that can distinguish between genuine genomic amplification and cDNA contamination.
