The Gaps

What Primer3 Does Not Do

Primer3 solves one problem well — picking primers from a template. It was never designed to validate those primers for real-world laboratory use. Here is what every researcher still has to do manually after Primer3 finishes.

🔬

No Assay Validation

Primer3 does not check specificity against the genome, does not screen for known SNPs at the binding site, and does not evaluate secondary structure formation. You get primers, not a validated assay.

📄

No Audit Report

There is no downloadable report documenting what was checked, what passed, and what failed. Regulated labs and clinical research environments require an audit trail. Primer3 does not produce one.

🧪

No Multiplex Support

Designing primers for a multiplex PCR reaction involves cross-dimer compatibility checks across all primer pairs. Primer3 treats each primer pair independently. Cross-reactivity goes undetected.

22-Step Pipeline

What VigyanLLM Adds Beyond Primer Picking

The 22-step VigyanLLM pipeline uses Primer3 thermodynamics as a foundation. Every subsequent step is a validation gate that a free tool does not provide.

Specificity (BLAST + Bowtie2)

Every candidate primer pair is searched against NCBI nt/nr and a local Bowtie2 index. Off-target hits with >80% homology are flagged. You see exactly where else your primers might bind before you order them.

SNP Filter (dbSNP Overlap)

Primer binding sites are checked against dbSNP. If a validated SNP falls within a primer footprint, the pair is downgraded. This prevents amplification failure caused by single-nucleotide mismatches in the target population.

Secondary Structure (Hairpin & Dimer)

SantaLucia nearest-neighbour thermodynamics is used to compute hairpin (ΔG > −2.0 kcal/mol), self-dimer (ΔG > −5.0), and cross-dimer (ΔG > −5.0) stability. Primers that form stable dimers at assay temperature are rejected.

Repeat Masking & Manufacturing Check

Simple repeats, homopolymer runs (>4 nt), and low-complexity regions are masked before design. A manufacturing compatibility check flags sequences with high GC skew, long homopolymer stretches, or predicted synthesis failure.

Multiplex Compatibility

All primer pairs are evaluated in combination. Cross-pair dimers, Tm spread, and amplicon size overlap are scored. The pipeline ranks multiplex panels by overall conflict score so you can pick the safest combination.

PDF Audit Report

Every design run produces a downloadable PDF with pass/fail results for each of the 22 checks, primer sequences, thermodynamic metrics, BLAST alignments, SNP annotations, and a composite assay confidence score. Ready for lab notebooks and regulatory review.
