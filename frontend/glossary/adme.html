ADME

Drug Discovery

Definition

ADME (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion) describes pharmacokinetic drug behavior: absorption into circulation, tissue distribution, hepatic CYP450 metabolism, and renal/hepatic excretion, essential for drug development and dosing.

Detailed Explanation

The four pharmacokinetic processes determining drug behavior in the body: Absorption (how the drug enters systemic circulation), Distribution (how it spreads to tissues), Metabolism (enzymatic transformation, primarily by CYP450 enzymes in the liver), and Excretion (elimination via kidneys, liver, or other routes). ADME profiling is essential in drug development to predict bioavailability, half-life, drug-drug interactions, and dosing regimens.
