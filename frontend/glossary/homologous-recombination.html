homologous recombination

Molecular Biology

Definition

Homologous recombination exchanges genetic information between similar DNA sequences, producing crossover diversity during meiosis and accurately repairing double-strand breaks using sister chromatid templates. CRISPR gene editing often exploits this mechanism.

Detailed Explanation

A DNA repair and genetic exchange mechanism in which two DNA molecules with similar (homologous) sequences exchange genetic information. During meiosis, homologous recombination between homologous chromosomes produces crossover events that increase genetic diversity. In DNA repair, it uses an undamaged sister chromatid as a template to accurately repair double-strand breaks. CRISPR-mediated gene editing often relies on homologous recombination to insert donor DNA templates.
