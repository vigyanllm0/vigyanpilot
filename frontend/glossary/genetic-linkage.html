genetic linkage

Molecular Biology

Definition

Genetic linkage is the co-inheritance tendency of nearby genes on the same chromosome during meiosis, measured in centimorgans (1% recombination = 1 cM), used for gene mapping and positional cloning.

Detailed Explanation

The tendency of genes or DNA sequences located close together on the same chromosome to be inherited together during meiosis. Linked genes do not assort independently (violating Mendel's law of independent assortment). The degree of linkage is measured by recombination frequency: 1% recombination = 1 centimorgan (cM) distance. Genetic linkage maps are constructed using recombination data and used for gene mapping and positional cloning.
