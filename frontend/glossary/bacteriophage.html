bacteriophage

Virology

Definition

Bacteriophages are viruses infecting bacteria (~10^31 on Earth) with applications in phage therapy (antibiotic alternative), molecular tools (M13 phage display, lambda cloning), and CRISPR systems derived from phage defense mechanisms.

Detailed Explanation

A virus that infects and replicates within bacteria, also called phage. Bacteriophages are the most abundant biological entities on Earth (~10^31 particles). Phages have protein capsids enclosing DNA or RNA genomes and recognize specific bacterial surface receptors. Applications include phage therapy (alternative to antibiotics), molecular biology tools (M13 phage display, lambda phage cloning vectors), and CRISPR-Cas systems (derived from phage defense mechanisms).
