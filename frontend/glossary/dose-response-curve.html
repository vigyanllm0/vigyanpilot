dose-response curve

Drug Discovery

Definition

The dose-response curve graphs drug concentration versus pharmacological effect, with key parameters EC50 (effective concentration), IC50 (inhibitory concentration), and Emax (maximum effect), fundamental to pharmacology and drug discovery.

Detailed Explanation

A graphical representation of the relationship between drug dose (or concentration) and the pharmacological effect produced. Key parameters include EC50 (half-maximal effective concentration), IC50 (half-maximal inhibitory concentration), and Emax (maximum effect). Dose-response relationships are fundamental to pharmacology, toxicology (LD50), and drug discovery for characterizing compound potency and therapeutic windows.
