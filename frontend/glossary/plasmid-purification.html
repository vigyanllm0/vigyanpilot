plasmid purification

Microbiology

Definition

Plasmid purification isolates plasmid DNA from bacteria via alkaline lysis and column purification at mini/midi/maxi-prep scales, producing plasmid DNA for sequencing, transfection, in vitro transcription, and DNA construct storage.

Detailed Explanation

The isolation of plasmid DNA from bacterial cells, typically using alkaline lysis followed by column-based purification. Mini-prep (small scale, 1-5 mL culture), midi-prep (medium scale), and maxi-prep (large scale) protocols yield plasmid DNA of varying purity and quantity. Purified plasmids are used for sequencing, transfection, in vitro transcription, and long-term storage of cloned DNA constructs.
