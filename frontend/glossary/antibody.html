antibody

Immunology

Definition

Antibodies are Y-shaped immunoglobulins produced by B cells that bind specific antigens. Five classes (IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, IgD) serve different immune functions. They are essential biotechnology tools for ELISA, Western blot, and therapeutic monoclonal antibodies.

Detailed Explanation

A Y-shaped immunoglobulin protein produced by B cells of the adaptive immune system that binds specifically to antigens (foreign molecules). Antibodies consist of two heavy chains and two light chains with variable regions determining antigen specificity. Five classes (IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, IgD) have different functions. Antibodies are essential tools in biotechnology: ELISA, Western blot, immunohistochemistry, flow cytometry, and therapeutic monoclonal antibodies.
