prime editing

Genome Editing

Definition

Prime editing performs all 12 point mutations, insertions, and deletions without DSBs or donor templates using Cas9 nickase-reverse transcriptase fusion with pegRNA, offering higher precision and broader scope than base or standard CRISPR editing.

Detailed Explanation

A versatile genome editing technique that can perform all 12 types of point mutations, small insertions, and small deletions without double-strand breaks or donor DNA templates. Prime editing uses a Cas9 nickase fused to reverse transcriptase and a prime editing guide RNA (pegRNA) encoding the desired edit. It offers higher precision than base editing with broader editing scope than standard CRISPR-Cas9, making it a powerful tool for functional genomics and gene therapy.
