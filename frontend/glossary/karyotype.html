karyotype

Genetics & Genomics

Definition

Detailed Explanation

The complete set of chromosomes in a cell, organized by size, banding pattern, and centromere position. A karyotype is visualized during metaphase of cell division and used clinically to detect chromosomal abnormalities such as aneuploidy (trisomy 21/Down syndrome), deletions, duplications, translocations, and inversions. Human karyotype: 46 chromosomes (22 pairs autosomes + XY or XX sex chromosomes).
