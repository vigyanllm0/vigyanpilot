What Makes VigyanLLM a Biomedical AI Platform?

VigyanLLM is not a wrapper around third-party APIs. It is an integrated biomedical AI platform that combines multi-agent orchestration, biophysical simulation, and laboratory-grade validation into a single, self-hosted environment. The platform is designed for researchers who need reproducibility, auditability, and data sovereignty — without compromising on analytical depth.

At its core, VigyanLLM runs a 22-step primer and probe design pipeline that covers gene fetch, Primer3 integration, specificity screening against the reference genome, secondary structure prediction using SantaLucia unified nearest-neighbour thermodynamics, SNP filtering, repeat masking, and multiplex compatibility checks. Each output is accompanied by an audit-ready HTML report suitable for lab notebooks and regulatory review.

Beyond primer design, the platform provides a growing suite of biomedical AI capabilities including molecular docking analysis, CRISPR off-target scoring, PCR simulation, and sequence alignment — all accessible through a unified interface. These tools are backed by the same multi-agent pipeline engine that ensures every computation is deterministic and fully reproducible.

Sovereign and On-Premises Architecture

Data sovereignty is a first-class requirement in biomedical research. Genomic sequences, patient-derived data, and proprietary assay designs must never leave the institutional network. VigyanLLM is built from the ground up as an on-premises-first platform. Every component — from the gene fetch module to the report generator — runs locally with no external API calls required.

This architecture eliminates the compliance and security risks associated with cloud-only platforms. Research labs handling sensitive datasets under GDPR, HIPAA, or institutional review board protocols can deploy VigyanLLM on their own hardware, configure network policies, and maintain full control over data access and retention. The platform supports Linux and macOS environments with a straightforward installation process.

For labs that prefer a managed deployment, VigyanLLM offers a hybrid model where the pipeline engine runs on-premises while optional telemetry and update checks can be disabled entirely. There is no vendor lock-in, no data egress fees, and no requirement to share experimental data with third-party servers.

Tools Available on the Platform

Primer Design Pipeline

22-step biophysical validation with Primer3, BLAST specificity, thermodynamics, SNP filters, repeat masking, and multiplex checks. Supports single-plex and multiplex designs.

qPCR Probe Design

TaqMan and hydrolysis probe design with Tm matching, amplicon size optimisation, and secondary structure avoidance. Full validation reports included.

Molecular Docking

Protein-ligand docking with scoring, pose visualisation, and batch analysis for drug discovery workflows.

CRISPR Off-Target Scoring

Guide RNA design with genome-wide off-target prediction, mismatch tolerance analysis, and ranked candidate output.

PCR Simulation

In silico PCR amplification simulation with product size prediction, melting temperature profiles, and cross-amplification checks.

Sequence Alignment

Pairwise and multiple sequence alignment with visual output, conservation scoring, and primer binding site annotation.

Target Users and Use Cases

VigyanLLM serves a broad range of users in the life sciences. Academic research labs use the platform for PCR assay development, cloning primer design, and CRISPR guide validation. Core facilities and institutional genomics centres deploy VigyanLLM as a shared service for their research communities, providing consistent, auditable primer design across multiple projects.

Pharmaceutical R&D teams leverage the platform for high-throughput assay development, biomarker validation, and quality-controlled primer batch design. The platform's deterministic pipelines ensure that designs are reproducible across sites and over time — a critical requirement for regulated environments and multi-centre studies.

Contract research organisations (CROs) use VigyanLLM to standardise primer design workflows across client projects, with each design accompanied by a comprehensive validation report suitable for client delivery. The on-premises deployment model ensures that client data remains segregated and secure within the CRO's infrastructure.
