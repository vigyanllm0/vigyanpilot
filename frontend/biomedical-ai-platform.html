Sovereign AI · On-Premise · No External API

Biomedical AI Platform for Research Labs

VigyanLLM is a sovereign biomedical AI platform that combines validated primer design, consensus molecular docking, CRISPR sgRNA analysis, and clinical reasoning — all running on your infrastructure with zero external API calls.

What Is a Sovereign Biomedical AI Platform?

A sovereign biomedical AI platform is an artificial intelligence system for life sciences research that operates entirely within an organization's controlled infrastructure. Unlike cloud-based tools that send your data to external servers, sovereign AI ensures:

  • Data residency: All genomic, proteomic, and clinical data remains within your institution
  • Regulatory compliance: Meets DPDP Act 2023 and GDPR requirements
  • Security: No external API calls means no data exposure to third parties
  • Availability: Works offline in secure facilities with air-gapped networks
  • Customization: Adapt the AI to your specific research workflows

The VigyanLLM Platform: Four Integrated Modules

Module 1: VigyanLLM Primer — Validated Primer & Probe Design

A 24-step validation pipeline for PCR primer and TaqMan probe design. Features include:

  • Primer3-style design with extended validation
  • Thermodynamic checking (SantaLucia NN model with salt/Mg corrections)
  • BLAST + Bowtie2 specificity verification
  • dbSNP and ClinVar screening
  • Repeat masking (Dfam database)
  • Multiplex compatibility scoring
  • Audit-ready PDF, CSV, JSON reports
  • IDT and Twist vendor exports

Module 2: Consensus Molecular Docking

Multi-engine docking pipeline that runs Smina, Vina, and Gnina in parallel, then scores by structural consensus:

  • Parallel execution of 3 docking engines
  • Binding affinity aggregation (kcal/mol)
  • RMSD-based pose ranking
  • Consensus scoring for top-binding conformations
  • ESMFold preprocessing for apo structures
  • Output as ranked SDF/PDBQT file sets

Module 3: CRISPR sgRNA Analysis

Guide RNA design and validation for CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing:

  • On-target efficiency scoring (Doench 2016, Moreno-Mateos 2015)
  • Off-target prediction (CFD score, MIT specificity score)
  • PAM site identification (NGG, NAG, NNG)
  • Guide RNA secondary structure checking
  • Poly-T tract avoidance
  • Seed region GC content optimization

Module 4: Clinical Reasoning Engine

Pipeline-driven analysis for biomedical research applications:

  • Adverse drug reaction (ADR) detection
  • Drug interaction analysis
  • Literature mining for evidence synthesis
  • Case report form analysis
  • Research-use-only decision support
