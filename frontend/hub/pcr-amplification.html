Frequently Asked Questions

What are the different types of PCR?

Common PCR variants include: Standard PCR (endpoint amplification for genotyping and cloning), qPCR/Real-Time PCR (quantitative measurement of DNA amount during amplification), RT-PCR (reverse transcription PCR for RNA analysis), Multiplex PCR (simultaneous amplification of multiple targets), Bisulfite PCR (methylation analysis after sodium bisulfite treatment), Touchdown PCR (gradient annealing for improved specificity), Hot-Start PCR (reduced non-specific amplification), and Digital PCR (absolute quantification without standard curves).
