How Transcription Works

During transcription, RNA polymerase reads the DNA template strand and synthesizes a complementary RNA strand. The base pairing rules are: A→U, T→A, C→G, G→C. The resulting RNA transcript uses uracil (U) instead of thymine (T).

Frequently Asked Questions About DNA to RNA Converter

Everything you need to know about using this tool

Is the DNA to RNA Converter free?

Yes, the DNA to RNA transcription converter is completely free with no usage limits. No login, account, or API key required.

How does the transcription work?

The tool replaces thymine (T) with uracil (U) following the rules: A→U, T→A, C→G, G→C. The result is a complementary RNA transcript ready for downstream applications.

What is the difference between DNA and RNA?

DNA uses thymine (T) and is double-stranded. RNA uses uracil (U) instead of thymine and is typically single-stranded. During transcription, RNA polymerase synthesizes RNA from a DNA template.

Can I convert long sequences?

Yes, the tool accepts sequences of any length with no artificial limits on sequence size.

Is my sequence stored?

No. All conversion happens in your browser. Sequences are never sent to a server or stored anywhere.
