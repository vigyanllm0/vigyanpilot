CYP2D6 Primer Design

PCR primer design guide for the CYP2D6 (Cytochrome P450 2D6) gene located on chromosome 22q13. CYP2D6 encodes a highly polymorphic cytochrome P450 enzyme that metabolizes ~25% of all prescribed drugs including tamoxifen and codeine. Pre-configured parameters, SNP considerations, and validated primer design for CYP2D6 analysis.

About CYP2D6

The CYP2D6 gene (Cytochrome P450 2D6) on chromosome 22q13 The CYP2D6 locus displays extreme copy number variation (0–13+ copies) and sequence variants defining poor to ultrarapid metabolizer phenotypes. Key targets include Exons 1–9 for comprehensive mutation screening.

Mutations: *4 (rs3892097), *10 (rs1065852), gene duplication, deletion | GC content: 55% | Target exons: Exons 1–9

CYP2D6 Primer Design Challenges

  • Copy number variation: Full-gene deletions, duplications, and multiplications require comparative quantification methods alongside SNP genotyping
  • Hybrid gene rearrangements: CYP2D6-CYP2D7 hybrid genes from structural variation complicate amplification specificity
  • High homology with CYP2D7 pseudogene: 97% identity in exons 6–9 requires long-range or nested PCR for specific amplification

Key SNPs to Avoid in Primer Binding Sites

When designing CYP2D6 primers, avoid these clinically significant variants:

  • rs3892097 (*4 (splice)) — Exon 4 splicing defect, poor metabolizer
  • rs1065852 (*10 (P34S)) — Exon 1 reduced function, Asian population
  • rs16947 (*2 (R296C)) — Exon 6 common variant, normal function

Clinical Validation Required
All CYP2D6 primers designed with VigyanLLM are for research use only. Clinical diagnostic applications require additional wet-lab validation, Sanger sequencing confirmation, and regulatory approval before patient use.
