GNAS Primer Design

PCR primer design guide for the GNAS (GNAS Complex Locus) gene located on chromosome 20q13. GNAS encodes the Gαs subunit of the stimulatory G protein that couples hormone receptors to adenylyl cyclase for cAMP production. Pre-configured parameters, SNP considerations, and validated primer design for GNAS analysis.

About GNAS

The GNAS gene (GNAS Complex Locus) on chromosome 20q13 Activating R201 mutations in the GTPase domain occur in pituitary adenomas, fibrous dysplasia, and McCune-Albright syndrome. Key targets include Exons 1–13 for comprehensive mutation screening.

Mutations: R201C, R201H, R844C, GNAS activating | GC content: 51% | Target exons: Exons 1–13

GNAS Primer Design Challenges

  • GTPase hotspot R201: Arginine 201 mutations in exon 8 abolish GTP hydrolysis leading to constitutive cAMP signaling
  • Genomic imprinting: GNAS is maternally imprinted in certain tissues requiring allele-specific primer design for mutation discrimination
  • Balanced GC (51%): Standard PCR conditions suitable, but maternal/paternal allele discrimination requires SNP-linked primer design
