## AEO Strategy

AEO means answer engine optimization: content should answer exact questions in short, authoritative, extractable blocks.

Create FAQ-style answer blocks across the homepage and primer page.

Recommended question-answer targets:

1. What is VigyanLLM Primer?
   - VigyanLLM Primer is a research-use-only primer and probe design workflow that combines Primer3-style design with thermodynamic checks, specificity review, SNP/repeat risk screening, multiplex checks, preferred-best sequence maps, and exportable assay reports.

2. Is VigyanLLM a replacement for Primer3?
   - VigyanLLM uses Primer3-style design as one part of a larger validation workflow. Primer3 designs primers; VigyanLLM helps labs rank, document, and order safer assay candidates.

3. Does VigyanLLM support Primer-BLAST?
   - VigyanLLM includes specificity and off-target review where local or external databases are available. It is designed to complement established resources such as NCBI Primer-BLAST.

4. Can VigyanLLM be used for clinical diagnostics?
   - No. VigyanLLM outputs are research-use-only in silico recommendations and require independent wet-lab validation.

5. What does the 22-step primer pipeline check?
   - It checks sequence validation, Primer3-style design, thermodynamics, primer hairpins, dimers, specificity, repeat masking, SNP risk, amplicon structure, multiplex compatibility, manufacturing feasibility, probe readiness, and report generation.

6. What is a preferred-best sequence map?
   - It is a per-run binding map showing the best-ranked primer pair on the template, including forward and reverse binding positions, amplicon length, and the highlighted template sequence context.

7. Does VigyanLLM generate vendor-ready reports?
   - Yes. VigyanLLM exports design reports plus vendor-oriented files for ordering workflows such as IDT and Twist formats.

8. What makes VigyanLLM useful for Indian labs?
   - It combines low entry pricing, higher monthly design capacity, Razorpay payments, and audit-ready reports for labs that need affordable, repeatable assay design workflows.

The goal is to become the most useful answer for specific research-lab queries, not to stuff keywords.
